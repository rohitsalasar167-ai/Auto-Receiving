About Share %

If your master data has Share % = 60 and the master premium is ₹60, then your actual 100% premium is ₹100 (60 ÷ 0.60). When the commission statement shows ₹100, the app automatically calculates the full premium from your share and matches it correctly.

Share % Premium Logic

If master record has Share % set, the app calculates: Full Premium = Master Premium ÷ (Share% / 100). This full premium is compared against the statement premium. Example: Master OD = ₹60, Share = 60% → Full OD = ₹100. Statement shows ₹100 → Premium match ✓

Customer Name Cleaning

Before comparing names, the engine strips: Mr, Mrs, Ms, Dr, Prof, M/s, M/S, Ltd, Limited, Pvt, Private, Pvt. Ltd., LLC, LLP, Inc, Corp, Co., & Sons, & Co, Brothers, Enterprises, Industries, Traders, Agency, Services, Group, Holdings — and common salutations in Indian names. Then fuzzy token matching compares the core name words.

Premium — OD / TP / Total

The engine checks all three premiums independently: OD, TP, and Total. If any one of them matches between the statement and master (after applying share %), it counts as a premium match. This handles cases where the insurer reports only OD or only TP in the statement.
