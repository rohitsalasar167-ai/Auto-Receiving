Match Rules

How the matching engine decides which master record belongs to each commission row.

100% Match
Policy No + Customer Name + Premium + (Start Year or End Year)
All four criteria agree. Definite match — C.No and Inst No filled with full confidence.
Partial — High
Policy No + Premium + Year
Strong match — policy number and premium agree with year validation. Customer name may differ slightly.
Partial — Mid
Policy No + Endorsement No + Year  ·  or  ·  Endorsement No + Premium + Year
Good match for endorsement rows when both identifiers agree.
Partial — Low
Policy No + Year  ·  or  ·  Customer Name + Premium + Year (when no Policy No in master)
Weaker match — needs manual review but likely correct.
Unmatched
Year mismatch on all candidates, or no match found
Even if Policy No and Premium match, a year mismatch means wrong policy year — rejected to prevent bad picks.
Skipped (Master)
Brok Due within ±5 of zero
Records with near-zero brokerage are excluded from the matching pool entirely.
Inst No / Endorsement Separation

Inst No = 0 (base policies) only match statement rows with no endorsement number.
Inst No > 0 (endorsements) only match statement rows that carry an endorsement number. The two pools never cross-match — a base policy will never accidentally match an endorsement row.

Year / Date Logic

The year is extracted from Policy Start Date and Policy End Date on both sides. At least one of the two years must agree between the statement row and the master record. Handles dd/mm/yyyy, yyyy-mm-dd, and Excel serial date formats automatically.
